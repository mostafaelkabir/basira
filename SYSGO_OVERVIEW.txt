SYSGO — Product Overview
========================
For UX Design collaboration · May 2026


WHAT IS SYSGO?
--------------
SysGo is a personal accountability app built around one idea: your goals should
drive your daily actions. Most task managers treat everything as a flat list.
SysGo organizes your work around the type of goal it belongs to, and surfaces
the right things at the right time.

It's designed for one user — someone who has long-term ambitions, ongoing
projects, and daily habits, and wants one place to see all of it without
noise.


THE THREE GOAL TYPES
--------------------
Every goal in SysGo has a type, and the type determines how its tasks behave:

  RESOLUTION — A yearly commitment (e.g. "Get fit", "Read more").
    Tasks under resolutions are habits — they repeat indefinitely and are
    checked in daily. Progress is measured in streaks and weekly consistency,
    not completion. A habit is never "done", it just keeps going.

  PROJECT — A finite goal with a clear end (e.g. "Launch website", "Write report").
    Tasks have a start, and they complete. You can require proof of completion
    (a photo, a note, a file) to keep yourself honest.

  DAILY — Everyday operational tasks (e.g. "Reply to emails", "Review calendar").
    These are recurring todos that persist until completed or removed. No habit
    tracking, no proof required — just get it done.

Goals can also have cover images and icons to make them feel more personal
and motivating.


THE FOUR SCREENS
----------------

  1. TODAY
     The daily command center. Three sections:

     - Focus (top): Up to 3 tasks pinned as today's most important things.
       You pick them manually from your goals. Numbered 1–2–3 with a gold accent.

     - Plan: All your daily todos, plus any habits or project tasks you've
       explicitly added to today's plan. This is the full list of "what I'm
       doing today". You can quick-add todos inline, defer things to tomorrow,
       or pin something up to Focus.

     - Add from habits & projects: A collapsible picker to pull in specific
       habits or project tasks into today's plan without cluttering the view.

  2. GOALS
     A full list of all your goals, grouped by type (Resolutions, Projects,
     Daily). Tapping a goal opens its detail view where you can see all tasks,
     add new ones, set due dates, attach proof, and leave comments/notes on
     individual tasks.

  3. PROGRESS
     A weekly view of how you're actually doing. Two sections:

     - Goals grid: Every goal as a card showing momentum (when was the last
       activity?), and a progress bar. For resolutions this shows "X/N habits
       on track this week". For projects it shows task completion percentage.

     - Habits this week: Every habit with a 7-day dot grid (the last 7 days),
       a weekly fraction (e.g. 2/3 for a 3x/week habit), and a streak counter.
       Habits that have hit their weekly target are dimmed and moved to the
       bottom. Expanding a habit shows a 4-week heatmap.

  4. PEOPLE
     A lightweight relationship tracker. Store contacts, log calls/interactions,
     and add notes. Separate from goals — it's for maintaining relationships you
     care about.


KEY INTERACTIONS
----------------

  Checking in a habit:
    Tap the circle on any habit. If the habit requires proof, a modal appears
    asking for a note, photo, or file before the check-in is recorded.

  Completing a task:
    Tap the circle on any task. Same proof flow if proof is required.

  Pinning to Focus:
    From the Plan section or the Focus picker, tap the star/pin icon on any
    task to add it to today's Focus (max 3). Remove by tapping ✕.

  Deferring:
    Tasks can be deferred to tomorrow with one tap. They disappear from today
    and reappear the next day.

  Adding proof:
    Text note, image upload, or file attachment. Proof is attached to the task
    and visible in the activity feed below it.

  Comments:
    Any task can have comments — quick notes or updates that appear in a
    collapsible activity feed under the task.

  Weekly Review:
    Accessible from the Today header. Shows a summary of the past week —
    what got done, streaks, and momentum across goals.


VISUAL LANGUAGE (current)
--------------------------
  - Warm, neutral palette: sand tones, teal accents, gold highlights
  - Cards with subtle shadows and rounded corners throughout
  - Gold diamond (◆) used as section dividers
  - Color-coded momentum: green = active, gold = slowing, orange/red = stalled
  - Habit heatmaps inspired by GitHub's contribution graph
  - Cover images on goals replace the card background (full bleed with overlay)


WHAT'S NOT BUILT YET
---------------------
The longer-term vision is for SysGo to learn from your patterns and eventually
suggest realistic next steps toward your goals — an AI layer that knows your
history and helps you plan, not just track. The data model and task structure
are being built with that in mind.
